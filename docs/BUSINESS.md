# JoyVerse — Positioning & Value

*The non-technical case: who benefits, why it matters, and where it goes next.*

> JoyVerse is built to **help children**, not to make money. This document frames
> its value for the people who decide whether to use it — schools, clinics,
> therapists, and parents — and is honest about its current stage.

## The value proposition

**For children with dyslexia, JoyVerse turns necessary-but-repetitive literacy
practice into something they'll actually come back to — and it shows everyone
that it's working.**

Most phonics apps are a pile of games. JoyVerse's difference is the **loop around
the games**: it assesses where a child is, recommends what to practise, adapts as
they play, and then *measures and displays growth*. That measurable-growth story
is what turns "a fun app" into "a tool worth a specialist's time."

## Who benefits, and how

| Audience | What they get |
|---|---|
| **Children** | Friendly, adaptive practice across all core reading skills; visible progress and streaks that keep them motivated. |
| **Parents** | A plain-language weekly summary ("mastered 12 new sight words; reading speed up 12%"); confidence that it's safe (on-device, nothing recorded). |
| **Therapists / special educators** | Assign practice, monitor progress between sessions, see per-skill growth at a glance, and export reports — without extra admin. |
| **Schools** | A free, accessibility-first practice layer that complements structured-literacy instruction. |

## Differentiators

1. **Measurable growth, surfaced continuously** — not just scores, but
   baseline→now deltas for every skill.
2. **Privacy-first emotion sensing** — on-device, opt-in, nothing stored. Most
   "emotion AI" for kids can't say that.
3. **Evidence-aligned design** — mapped to structured-literacy skill domains,
   including RAN (a well-established reading-difficulty indicator). See
   [METHODOLOGY.md](METHODOLOGY.md).
4. **Accessibility built in** — OpenDyslexic font, adjustable text, reliable TTS.
5. **No special hardware or installs** — runs in a browser.

## What it is — and isn't (be honest in every conversation)

- ✅ **Is:** evidence-*aligned* practice + progress monitoring, to **complement**
  a specialist.
- ❌ **Is not** a diagnostic instrument. It does not diagnose dyslexia.
- ❌ **Is not** a replacement for a qualified specialist or structured-literacy
  curriculum.

Overclaiming ("clinically proven", "diagnoses dyslexia", "accurate emotion AI")
is the fastest way to lose credibility with an expert. Don't.

## Honest stage assessment

JoyVerse is an **advanced prototype / early pilot-ready product**:
- ✅ Feature-complete across the core skill domains, with progress tracking and a
  therapist view.
- ✅ Built privacy-first and accessibility-first.
- ⚠️ **No efficacy data yet** — growth is measured per-child, but there's no
  controlled study showing it improves outcomes versus a baseline.
- ⚠️ **No external clinical/content review yet.**
- ⚠️ **English-literacy only** — a real fit consideration in multilingual
  settings (see [PITCH.md](PITCH.md)).

## Roadmap (to strengthen the case)

1. **Run a pilot** at 1–2 institutions with consent — gather real pre/post growth
   data (the dashboard already produces it).
2. **Formal baseline assessment battery** — short, standardized timed RAN +
   phoneme + nonsense-word tasks with scheduled re-assessment.
3. **External structured-literacy review** of the scope & sequence and every item
   a child sees.
4. **Multilingual content** (e.g. Telugu/Hindi literacy) if targeting regional
   institutions.
5. **Accessibility & security audit** before any large-scale use.

## Sustainability

The product is free to use. Running costs are modest (a small Node host + a
MongoDB instance). It can run on free tiers for a pilot. No revenue model is
required for the current goal: getting it into the hands of children who benefit,
and learning from real use.
